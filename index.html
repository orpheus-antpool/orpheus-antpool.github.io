About

Greetings and thank you for considering this pool! We hope you will join our journey of building a community that supports the Cardano ecosystem through increased security, diversity and sustainability. Find out below why:

Security

Holding your ADA coins in your own wallet gives you direct ownership. This is a step in the right direction. But what about delegating? Unlike other blockchains, on Cardano your coins never leave your wallet, nor are they locked for the duration of the delegation. You can freely transfer, spend and change the delegation at any time. Your delegation simply means you entrust us with your voting power, which raises our chance to mint new blocks and earn rewards that we share with you. In other words, you can give us a chance to earn your trust totally risk-free. In turn, we use reliable hardware and secure links based on VPN port forwarding to secure your rewards by delivering a high service uptime and anonymized, encrypted network traffic that minimizes any attack exposure. By supporting us, you enfoce decentralization!

Balance

We are a small pool operator and have pleged to remain so by joining the Cardano Single Pool Alliance, which builds a more diverse and transparent network. Why does this matter? Big players can afford to run a large number of pools, which essentially centralizes the voting power into their hands, which makes it easier to influence how new blocks are minted on the blockchain. Furthermore, many big players are centralized exchanges that take a large share of the rewards but offer a small cut to their clients. By staking with us, you discourage them to take advantage of others. We have always applied a small 1% fee and pledge to keep applying the same fee in the future. We are not offering 0% fee promotions to attract a large number of delegations only to hike the fee later. By supporting us, you bring more balance and stability to the Cardano community!

Sustainability

We strive to be highly energy-efficient to dispell the myth that blockchains are bad for the environment. To this end, we run our pool on modern embedded hardware (RK3588 with 8x ARM cores and NVMe 3.0 supporting 2~GB/s I/O throughput) that draws less than 20W on the average for two relays and a block producer. This is a challening because the cardano node (core component to run the pool) is pre-build for x86 architectures. We always compile from source and fine-tune the code for ARM achitectures (including the Haskell garbage collector). Furthermore, we contribute open-source software to the Cardano community, both through the official developer portal (Cardano Light Tools) and by contributing to larger efforts (Guild Operators). Feel free to visit us on Github. By supporting us, you contribute to making Cardano more environmentally friendly!
